Account-based sales is a sales strategy that focuses on selling to specific target accounts or
organizations, rather than individual prospects. This approach is often used in B2B sales and is
designed to deliver a highly personalized and tailored sales experience to each target account.

In an account-based sales model, sales teams will conduct research to identify key decision-makers
within the target account and create a strategic plan to engage and build relationships with
those individuals. This can involve a variety of tactics, such as account-based marketing,
executive events, and personalized communication. The goal of an account-based sales strategy
is to create a highly relevant and valuable experience for the target account, which will
increase the chances of a successful sale.

This approach is becoming increasingly popular as B2B buyers become more savvy and expect a
higher level of personalization and engagement from sales teams. By focusing on a smaller
number of high-value accounts, sales teams can drive more revenue and create more efficient
and effective sales processes.
